                     FAQ by: Fire_Pro_Fan
__________________________________________________________________


Following parent company Human Entertainment's bankruptcy
in January of 2000, Japanese game developer Spike would
claim ownership of the Fire Pro Wrestling franchise and,
in less than seven months, release the last official Fire
Pro Wrestling game bearing Human's namesake on Bandai's
portable WonderSwan system.

In a nutshell: the game: Fire ProWrestling for WonderSwan,
was a direct port of Human's Super Fire ProWrestling X
Premium (released 4 years prior on Nintendo's Super
Famicom) with a handful of updates and new gameplay
features such as updated movesets, an improved roster,
a career management mode and even online play!

This FAQ contains:

* All 136 Wrestlers covered
* All finishers and secret moves listed
* Critical submission and K.O. moves
* Secrets, tips & unlockables 
* Save file download link

Please note: this FAQ is unfinished. To see what needs
to be added or to submit your own corrections, please
visit the "CONTACT" section at the end of this FAQ.

Tip: if using an Emulator,I highly suggest mapping
the Y1,Y2,Y3 and Y4 buttons as one single button (
it makes pulling off "Secret moves" much easier).


===============
While standing:
===============

X1 - Move up

X2 - Move right

X3 - Move down

X4 - Move left

To simplify things, I'll refer to all four X
buttons as Up, Down, Left and Right throughout
the rest of this FAQ.


B - Light Strike

A - Medium Strike

A + B - Strong strike


Y1 - No input

Y2 - Taunt 1

Y3 - No input

Y4 - Taunt 2

Y1 + Y2 + Y3 + Y4 - Secret strike


START - Run

START + Y1 - Pause game

START - Resume game


==============
While running:
==============

B - Light Strike

A - Medium Strike

A + B - Strong strike
(not every Wrestler can perform this move
while running)

Up/Down/Left/Right - Stop running

Button Mash - Escape opponent's Irish Whip


==========================
While opponent is running:
==========================

B - Light Strike/grapple

A - Medium Strike/grapple


=================
While locking up:
=================

Up/Down/Left/Right + B - Light grapple

Up/Down/Left/Right + A - Medium grapple

Up/Down/Left/Right + A + B - Strong grapple

Y1 + Y2 + Y3 + Y4 - Secret grapple

START - Irish Whip

Up/Down/Left/Right + START - Irish Whip in any direction
                             
Left/Right + START - Irish Whip opponent into turnbuckle
(must be standing in the middle of the ring)


=================================
While locking up behind opponent:
=================================

Up/Down/Left/Right + B - Light grapple

Up/Down/Left/Right + A - Medium grapple

Up/Down/Left/Right + A + B - Strong grapple

Y1 + Y2 + Y3 + Y4 - Secret grapple

START - Irish Whip

Up/Down/Left/Right + START - Irish Whip in any direction


=============================================
While opponent attempts a lockup from behind:
=============================================

B/A - Reverse 

A + B - Special reversal
(not every Wrestler can perform this attack)


===================================
While opponent is laying on ground:
===================================

A - Submission hold/ground strike

B - Pick up opponent (at head of downed opponent - face up)

B - Turn opponent over (at feet of downed opponent - face up)

B - Pick up opponent (at feet of downed opponent - face down)

A + B - Pin/submission hold (varies with each Wrestler) 


=======================
While laying on ground:
=======================

Button mash - escape pinfall/submission hold


===================
From the top ropes:
===================

Up + A - Climb top turnbuckle

Left + A/Right + A (climb left/right turnbuckle)

Down + A (climb bottom turnbuckle)

Down - Climb down from turnbuckle


B - Light Strike

A - Medium Strike

A + B - Strong strike

Y1 + Y2 + Y3 + Y4 - Secret attack


Up/Down/Left/Right + A/B - Throw opponent from turnbuckle

Up/Down/Left/Right + A/B - Turnbuckle grapple
(opponent must be Irish Whipped towards the
left or right turnbuckle first)


=========
Ringside:
=========

At least one Wrestler must be thrown out of the ring
before either Wrestler can enter/exit the ring

Up + Left/Right + B - Exit ring from top left/right

Down + Left/Right + B - Exit ring from bottom left/right

Up + Left/Right + A/B - Enter ring from bottom left/right

Down + Left/Right + B - Enter ring from top left/right


===================
Suicida techniques:
===================

Suicida:

While the opponent is outside the ring, approach
the nearby ring ropes (while still inside the ring)
and press A + B.

Running Suicida:

Shortly after the opponent is thrown outside the ring,
the player must begin running (while still inside the
ring) towards the ring ropes then pressing A, B or
A + B to initiate a Running Suicida. 

Not every Wrestler has a Suicida technique.

A,B, A + B - Suicida

**************MASTER OF THE RING*****************

=====================================================

Upon selecting MASTER OF RING mode, the following
options are presented:


 [MASTER] - Organize a series of Wrestling shows 
            that will be graded.

 [FREE] - Organize a series of Wrestling shows
          that will not be graded with customizable
          options

 [RANKING] - View high scores from MASTER.


____________________________________


In MASTER mode, the player takes the role of
a Booker who is put in charge of organizing a
series of shows for the World's biggest Pro-
Wrestling promotions.

There are a total of eight stages in MASTER mode.
Each stage representing a different Wrestling 
promotion, the promotion's roster and each
promotion's champion. 

As the Booker, it is the player's responsibility
to put on the greatest show possible which is
reflected in ticket sales for each event.

Each stage has a set minimum of ticket sales 
that the player is required to reach in order
to clear the stage. If the player fails to reach
the minimum number of ticket sales by the end
of the show, the player will receive a GAME OVER
and will be asked to restart the stage.

When first starting one of the eight stages, the
player is asked if they would like to include a
Championship match in their show.

 ___________________________________
|                                   |
| YES - Enables Championship match. |
| NO - Disables Championship match. |
|___________________________________|

When it comes to grading,the player will receive
better ticket sales if the Championship match
option is enabled.

The next question asks the player which of the
five rounds the player would like to have the
Championship fight on the card.

For the best ticket sale results, I suggest
selecting Round 5 as the Championship match.

If the player has created a Create-A-Wrestler,
the first question asked before starting a stage
will be:

 __________________________________
|                                  |
| YES - Allow Created Wrestlers.   |
| NO -  Disable Created Wrestlers. | 
|__________________________________|

For better marks, I recommend refraining from
using a Create-A-Wrestler in MASTER mode.

At the end of each show, the player will be
graded using the following criteria:


 ______________________
|                      |
|                      |
| MATCH MAKER   ____   |
|                      |
| GAME CONTENT  ____   |
|                      |
| ENTERTAINMENT ____   |
|     COLOR            |
|                      |
|______________________|

 _____________
|             |
| MATCH MAKER |
|_____________|

The Wrestlers chosen for the show and the
order in which they appear on the show's card.

The player is encouraged to create a realistic
Wrestling card with lesser known/rookie talent
starting the show in the first few rounds to
closing the show with each promotion's popular
main eventers.

It should be noted that this section counts
for roughly 80% of the player's final mark
at the end of the stage.
 ______________
|              |
| GAME CONTENT |
|______________|

The quality of each match on the card.

To get a higher mark in this section, both
Wrestlers must cooperate to put on a high
quality match. Squash matches, countouts,
disqualifications and one-sided matches
result in lower Game Content grades.
 _____________________
|                     |
| ENTERTAINMENT COLOR |
|_____________________|

This section grades just how entertaining
the show was based on the crowd's reaction
to each round.

Higher Entertainment Color marks come from:
* Peforming each Wrestler's finishing move
* Taunting often during the match
* Near pinfalls
* Special moves (A + B)


As for the grades themselves:
 ______________
|              |
| S - Perfect  |
| A - Great    |
| B - Good     |
| C - Bad      |
| D - Very Bad |
| E - Abysmal  |
|______________|

After completing Stage 7, the game's ending is shown
displaying a championship trophy, the player's total
number of ticket sales acquired through MASTER OF
RING and the game's credits.

Upon resetting the game after the credits have finished,
the "LEGEND" roster is then unlocked featuring a large
number of hidden Wrestlers.

Completing Stage 7 also unlocks an additional 20 edit
points which can be used to further customize a player's
Created Wrestler in EDIT mode.

Lastly, in MASTER OF RING mode, the STAGE SELECT option
is then unlocked, allowing the player to go through and
replay any of the first seven stages of MASTER OF RING
mode as well as selecting the special hidden bonus level:

Even after unlocking the hidden Wrestlers, edit points
and extra features, Fire Pro WonderSwan presents one
final challenge in the form of a MASTER OF RING bonus
stage.

With a minimum ticket goal of sixty-thousand attendees,
the player is expected to complete all five rounds with
near perfect grades.

It should also be noted that the champions of this stage
are not listed (even when enabling a championship match,
the player has to choose them manually).

Just like all other stages played through the STAGE
SELECT option, there is no continue feature. Once the 
player loses the stage, they'll be brought back to the
main menu to choose Stage 8 all over again.

To date, I have yet to complete this stage.

=====================================================


When first starting ELIMINATION MATCH, the
following options are then presented:

[START]: Begin the Elimination Match

____________________________________


======
START:
======

Once the standard match settings have
been confirmed, the player is asked
which type of Elimination will take place:

          [Survival](default)

   [Wrestlers change after each match]


____________________________________


==========
TEAM EDIT:
==========

With this option, the player has the ability
to select a team of five Wrestlers of their
choosing. It is possible to select the same
Wrestler multiple times.

Once all five Wrestlers have been selected, a
new screen is then presented where the player
must assign a name to their Elimination team:

____________________________________

[HIRAGANA 1]

[ROMAN]

[HIRAGANA 2]

          [TOURNAMENT NAME]    [EXIT]

____________________________________


After the player's Elimination team has been
named, the player is then brought to a save
screen. 

Pressing the A button will prompt a question
asking the player if they would like to save
their custom Elimination team.

Selecting [YES] will overwrite and save the
player's custom Elimination team.

Pressing B will present another question
asking if the player would like to return
to the main menu screen. Selecting [YES]
will return to the main menu while choosing
[NO] will keep the player on the save menu
screen. 

Up to five custom Elimination Teams can be
saved.

8) SAVE/LOAD:
=============

________________________________________


========
9) EXIT:
========

Upon choosing this option, the game will prompt
the player asking them if they would like to
go to the main menu (discarding all changes
not saved).

Selecting NO will return the player to EDIT mode
while selecting YES will bring the player to the
main menu.

**************SECRETS, TIPS & TRICKS*****************

=====================================================


============================
Unlock the hidden Wrestlers:
============================

Successfully complete Stage 7 of "MASTER
OF RING" mode under "MASTER" settings.

After the game credits have finished (and
then  resetting the game) the twenty-three
hidden Wrestlers of the "LEGEND" division
will then be unlocked.


========================================
Unlock Stage Select and 120 EDIT points:
========================================

Successfully complete Stage 7 of "MASTER OF
RING" mode under "MASTER" settings.

After resetting the game, the Stage Select
feature will now be added as a selectable
option in "MASTER OF RING" mode.

Beating Stage 7 will also unlock an additional
20 EDIT mode points which can be used towards
customizing a Created Wrestler.


===============
Unlock Stage 8:
===============

To unlock the hidden and final stage in "MASTER
OF RING" mode, successfully complete Stage 7 of
"MASTER OF RING" mode under "MASTER" setting.

After finishing the credits and resetting the
game, choose "MASTER OF RING" mode under the
newly added "STAGE SELECT" option then scroll
through the list of stages until Stage 8 is
presented.

Stage 8 is a dream-match style promotion that
features Wrestlers from New Japan and All-Japan
Pro Wrestling with an expected audience
attendance rate of 60,000.


================
Critical glitch:
================

Select a Wrestler with a Critical inducing
attack (K.O.) that forces the opponent to
fall towards the player after the move has
been input.

Continue to perform the attack until the 
sound effect of a Critical has been activated
then immediately run towards the opponent to
bump into them before they have a chance to 
hit the ground.

Instead of the opponent falling to the ground
for a K.O., the opponent will now remain standing;
unable to be knocked out until they are thrown to
the ground again.

If the opponent places the player in a submission
hold during this glitch (away from the ropes and
under regular settings) the player will receive
a submission based critical instead!


===========================
Hidden EDIT mode templates:
===========================

In EDIT mode under the costume edit option,
the following hidden Wrestler templates are
selectable:

132) Seiji Sakaguchi
133) Great Muta
134) Hayabusa
135) Unknown Masked Wrestler
