0. INTRODUCTION
===============
o This document lists all of the map levels that are built into some of the
  more popular commercial Doom/Doom II games, and provides quick-reference
  information on the number of "official" secret areas on each map, and the
  maximum scores a player can achieve for discovering them.

o In this document, "official secrets" are areas or items on a map that can
  affect a player's "secrets found" score, shown on the tally screens that
  typically appear when a player exits a map.  (Different Doom/Doom II game
  versions display this score as "SECRET" or "SECRETS".)

o Other computer-based editions of the PC/Macintosh Doom/Doom II games exist,
  however they are identical in most aspects to the original PC/Mac games, and
  so are generally not addressed in this document.  Other editions of Doom,
  Doom II, etc. which are not addressed here may be included in future updates.

o This document is 79 characters wide, and is best viewed in a monospaced font.
  The master HTML version is located here: http://classicdoom.com/maxsec.htm
  The master text version is located here: http://classicdoom.com/maxsec.txt

1. DOOM GAME SECRETS: GENERAL
=============================
o Not all hidden areas or items in a given Doom/Doom II game are considered
  "official" secrets.  Many maps have hidden or hard-to-reach areas and items
  that will not affect a player's secrets-found score.

o In general an "official" (score-affecting) secret area will be a specially
  flagged horizontal surface, such as the floor of a room or tunnel, a patch of
  ground, the narrow strip beneath the bottom edge of a door, the top of a step
  or pedestal, etc.  Credit for discovering an official secret is commonly
  given when the lower endpoint of the player character's axis (an imaginary
  vertical line running down through the center of the character) contacts that
  horizontal surface.

o Note that in some instances it may not actually be possible for the player
  character's axis to cross the perimeter of a particular secret area or touch
  its surface.  This problem can occur when a small or narrow secret area is
  occupied by a solid object, or is located too close to a wall or teleporter.
  In rare cases an official secret may be located in a completely inaccessible
  section of the map, or a 100% score may depend on a player reaching secret
  areas that do not actually exist on the map.

o In some Doom/Doom II games, some locations have multiple secret surfaces
  within them.  This can, in effect, give more value to specific items or
  areas.  For example, one hidden stairway in Ultimate Doom has over a dozen
  surfaces flagged as secret, which makes that one stairway account for more
  than 80 percent of that map's official secrets.

o In some Doom/Doom II games, some "official secret" areas are not actually
  hidden, and may even be located in parts of the map that a player character
  must pass through in order to reach an exit (or other goal).

o In certain Doom games the player character must actually pick up a "secret"
  item in order to be credited with discovering it; Simply walking into/across
  the space that the item occupies will not always affect the player's score.

o The original PC Doom/Doom II games will give a SECRET score of "0%" for maps
  that have no official secret areas.  Note that some "source ports" of the
  original PC game engines will give a SECRET score of "100%" for those maps.

o Some game-console versions of Doom/Doom II will give a score of "0%" for maps
  that have no hidden official secrets; other game-console editions will give a
  score of "100%" for such maps.

25. GLOSSARY
============
o Quick-reference glossary for terms used in this document:

  DEATHMATCH: Player-versus-player gameplay.  PC versions can include monsters.
  DOOM (also DOOM 1/DOOM I): First Doom game; PC Doom has three 9-map Episodes.
  DOOM GAME/GAMES (used generally): Any/all "Doom" games (Doom, Doom II, etc.).
  DOOM II (DOOM 2: HELL ON EARTH): PC Doom 1 sequel with new monsters, 32 maps.
  DOOM II GAMES: Generally, Doom II and Doom II-based products like Final Doom.
  E#M#: Episode and Mission (Map) designator for map levels in some Doom games.
  EPISODE: A group of map levels.  Most games end after an Episode's last map.
  FINAL DOOM: PC: Two 32-map Doom II games; PSX: One 30-map (3 Episode) game.
  GBA: Acronym used for Nintendo's portable "Game Boy Advance" gaming devices.
  IPHONE: Smartphone by Apple, with form and functions similar to iPod touch.
  IPHONE/IPOD/ITOUCH DOOM: Alternate terms for Doom for iPhone and iPod touch.
  IPOD TOUCH (also ITOUCH): Handheld multimedia touch-screen device by Apple.
  MAC: Common abbreviation indicating Apple Macintosh computers or Doom games.
  MAP LEVEL/MAP: "Map level" is used to distinguish "level" from "skill level".
  MASTER LEVELS: PC: 21 Master Levels CD maps; Some used in PSX and Xbox games.
  MASTER LEVELS CD: PC CD has map/file sets "Master Levels" and "Maximum Doom".
  MAXIMUM DOOM: Collection of 3,493 PC maps/files on the Master Levels CD-ROM.
  OFFICIAL SECRET: An area/item on a map that affects the secrets-found score.
  PC: Usually refers to PC Doom games and the equivalent Macintosh Doom games.
  PLUTONIA: PC: 32-map game in Final Doom; PSX: A 6-map Episode of Final Doom.
  PS: Prefix/acronym used generically for Sony PlayStation consoles or devices.
  PSX: An acronym commonly used for the original 1995 Sony PlayStation console.
  SNES (also SUPER NES): Acronym for the "Super Nintendo Entertainment System".
  TALLY SCREEN: Shows kills, items and secrets attained in the last map level.
  TNT/EVILUTION: PC: 32-map game in Final Doom; PSX: 11-map Final Doom Episode.
  (THE) ULTIMATE DOOM: Update/reissue of original Doom 1; includes extra maps.
  WAD: One or more map levels combined in a file for computer-based Doom games.
  XBOX 360 DOOM: Ultimate Doom for Xbox 360, downloadable via Xbox Live Arcade.
  XBOX 360 DOOM II: Xbox 360 Doom II package downloadable via Xbox Live Arcade.
  XBOX LIVE/XBOX LIVE ARCADE: Refers to online services for Xbox and Xbox 360.
  ZODIAC: Versatile, game-oriented Personal Digital Assistant, by Tapwave, Inc.
